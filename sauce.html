Iphones (2007-17)

    Released in November 2017, the iPhone X marked a revolutionary shift in Apple's smartphone design and technology. It featured a stunning 5.8-inch Super Retina display, extending from edge to edge and adopting an OLED screen for vibrant colors and true blacks. The iPhone X introduced the concept of a bezel-less display, eliminating the traditional home button and incorporating Face ID, Apple's facial recognition technology, for secure and convenient device unlocking. Powered by the A11 Bionic chip with a neural engine, the iPhone X delivered impressive performance and supported augmented reality applications. The dual-camera system on the back comprised a 12-megapixel wide-angle and telephoto lens, both with optical image stabilization, enabling advanced photography features like Portrait mode and 4K video recording at 60 frames per second. The iPhone X also embraced wireless charging, utilizing a glass back for both aesthetics and functionality. The device was constructed with a surgical-grade stainless steel frame, providing durability and a premium feel. The absence of the home button allowed for intuitive gestures, enhancing the user experience. With an IP67 water and dust resistance rating, the iPhone X offered improved durability. Its elegant design, innovative features, and emphasis on facial recognition as the primary means of authentication solidified the device's position as a flagship model, influencing subsequent iPhone iterations. The iPhone X marked a milestone in Apple's design philosophy, setting the stage for the evolution of future iPhones.
